## About Me

</div>

![My Avatar](assets/images/avatar.jpg)

> **"Boundaries are not endings, but beginnings."**

<div align="center">


</div>

At twenty, standing at the intersection of two worlds—on one side, the miracle of cell division under the microscope; on the other, the rhythm of code flowing across the screen.

---

### Philosophy

In my world, two rivers converge:

Four bases writing life's most ancient program, each pair a carefully designed function call

Between 0 and 1 flows the pulse of a new era, every loop a rhythm of breath

**Pen is Heart's Extension**, writing from the heart, driving the pen with intention. Text is not a tool, but the soul's breath, the projection of thought onto paper

**Engineering is Thought Incarnate**, the projection of theory into reality. With code and steel, we forge abstract ideas into tangible truth

When the microscope's eyepiece meets the display's glow, I see:

One river flowing for four billion years, from single cell to human, from chaos to consciousness;
One river flowing for half a century, yet reaching the stars, from computation to creation.

The true miracle happens in that boundary-blurring, collision-stirring sea. There, biology meets code, reason meets creation, known meets unknown.

---

### Reflections

> "The starry heavens above and the moral law within, worth contemplating."

Kant said, two things fill the mind with ever-increasing awe—
**The starry heavens above**: The vastness and order of the universe, the motion and laws of all things
**The moral law within**: The depth and radiance of humanity, the freedom and dignity of the soul

Between exploring life's mysteries and writing code, I often pause to gaze at the stars and examine my heart. Technology can reach the stars, but only reflection lets the soul take root.
